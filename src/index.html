Invention - % based chance of success will vary.

In order to get the best chance of invention success, you need to first make a single copy of an owned blueprint with multiple runs. Do as many copy runs of different products as your slots permit. As for how many runs, you must strike a balance of what you can handle on the production line for your invention success. For example, if you make a 100-run copy of an 'Adaptive Invulnerability Field', you must be prepared to purchase the required amount of datacores x 100. The cost of production can run out of control really fast. If you did have 100x runs worth of datacores, you just might end up with 70x 10-run 'Adaptive Invulerability Field II' blueprint copies (depends on yoru invention success). Purchasing all the materials to make a total of 700x 'Adaptive Invulnerability Field IIs' can create a HUGE production cost up front. I generally do 20x-run copies for modules and 10x-run copies for ships.

Datacores. You need datacores of the appropriate science and racial variety to invent. You can farm them yourself or buy them from Jita. This is the first portion of the cost of your production run.

Skills. To even attempt an invention, your builder must have trained the appropriate science and racial skills. Training all of them to LV4 shouldn't be too great a task, and should yield a decent invention chance of success (generally 30%-45%).

Gather Materials. - because it's time to build.

Once you have successful inventions, single 10x production-run Blueprint Copies (BPCs) will appear in your hangar upon delivery based on your invention success. For example, let's say you made a single BPC of 'Adaptive Invulerability Field I' with 20 runs. You ran the 'Invent' action and had a success of 11 out of the 20 BPCs you invented on. You will receive 11x 10-run 'Adaptive Invulnerability Field II's which will give you a total of 110 'Adaptive Invulerability Field II's to build.

When you are building just 1 thing, the production UI is all you need. But if you want to invent on 15 other things, you have to figure out if you are going to build the sub-components or just buy all components from Jita. If you choose the latter, I guarantee that you will just break even on production because your cost of production will equal what you sell your goods for.

So now you need a way to aggregate all common materials (what the production calculator on this site does). Tech II products will have various input requirements, but they all mostly need 1) the original T1 version of the item, 2) The advanced components, and 3) planetary commodities. So you need to put all that on your shopping list too. When you calculate your profit margin (PM), hopefully you can have something in positive territory like > 20%.

To increase that PM, you must obviously produce as much of your own material as you can. You can save quite a bit by building your own T1 items, farming your planets for commodities, and mining your own morphite. You can also achieve significant production costs by building the sub-components. For example, if you need 1000x 'Sustained Shield Emitters' (advanced T2 component), you can purchase the BPO for them and build yourself. Now you need the advanced moon goo products that they are made of. The advanced moon goo products is a significant, but unavoidable cost of production. Unless your alliance/coalition has a nice exchange of local producers, Jita is really your only choice for material shopping.

The Pipeline. Keep track of what your building now, what you already have on market and what you want to build next.

This is really your production plan. My workflow is like this: 1) Do market research to figure out what your region of space needs. Eve Marketer is an excellent resource. 2)Start Blueprint copies of products I know are high in demand, but scarce on the market. If you think your region can use something, double-check your local trading hubs to ensure there aren't 1,000s of them. This will cause you to drive your selling price down due to local competition (and destroy your hard-earned PM!). 3) Using the Pipeline feature of this site, I can see my open orders on the market, do a quick PM analysis on the thing I want to make, track what I already have in production, and ear-mark products I want to invent on. The pipeline calculates isk values for everything and can tell you the value of future production.

If your going to produce 25 different items, make sure you can handle 25 simultaneous market orders. This means more skilling. Putting lots of things on the market ensures that you always have a steady stream of income. Character alts can easily handle the task of keeping things on the market.

Finally, the .01 cent game is all you need do to ensure that your product is 1st on the list in the local station. The .01 cent game is this: Find out what the lowest price of a particular item is on the market and sell your item for just .01 less. Admittedly this can be time-consuming if you have >50 items on the market and you have to adjust each one.

Now go and PVP to your heart's desire because while you are camping that gate, crushing a structure, or roaming with your buddies, you will have a constant stream of income from your market orders.
